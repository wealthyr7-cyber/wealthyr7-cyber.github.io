Salesforce Case Study

Interview Tracker System

A Salesforce app for managing job applications, interview rounds, structured feedback, and follow-up tasks. Built in Salesforce Developer Edition as part of an Admin learning path.

Business Problem

Job seekers and hiring teams often lose track of multiple applications, interview stages, and feedback spread across email and spreadsheets. There is no single view that ties status, notes, and upcoming actions together.

Solution Approach

Designed a four-object data model that keeps applications, interviews, and feedback connected, with master-detail relationships for lifecycle-dependent records and lookup relationships where independence is needed (like follow-up tasks).

Technical Implementation

Data Architecture: ├─ Account (Standard) → Companies ├─ Contact (Standard) → Recruiters / Hiring Managers ├─ Job Application (Custom) │ ├─ Master-Detail → Interview (cascade delete) │ │ └─ Master-Detail → Interview Feedback │ └─ Lookup → Follow-Up Task (independent) Objects Created: 3 custom objects Fields Created: 20+ custom fields Relationships: 2× Master-Detail, 1× Lookup Validation: * Interview Date required when Status is “Scheduled/Completed”. Features: * Multi-stage interview tracking * Structured feedback with ratings * Status workflow for applications and interviews

System Architecture

Data Model · Relationships · UX

The Interview Tracker is designed around a simple goal: keep every interview connected to a specific application and company, while making it easy to scan status and next actions from a single interface.

High-Level Experience

The main app surface is a list of job applications, each with key fields like role, company, location, current stage, and next interview date. From there, users can drill into related records (interviews, feedback, tasks) without losing context.

Why this Architecture?

  • Master-Detail from Interview to Job Application ensures that interviews cannot exist without an associated application and are cleaned up automatically when an application is deleted.
  • Master-Detail from Interview Feedback to Interview keeps all feedback tightly bound to a specific conversation and stage.
  • Lookup relationships are used when records need to live independently (for example, generic follow-up tasks or cross-application summaries).

Together, this structure keeps the org clean and easy to report on, while giving a clear drill-down path from high-level pipeline views to individual interview outcomes.
